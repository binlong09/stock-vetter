### What the disagreements mean

The most important disagreement between the analyst card and the primary-source check is on capital allocation. The analyst frames recent buybacks as value-accretive because the stock is 'depressed'; the primary-source check reaches the opposite conclusion because the buyback program in aggregate — including prior years at 40-45x earnings — destroyed capital at scale, and the fiscal 2025 program exceeded FCF and required incremental debt. When sources disagree on a fact like the average repurchase price vs. intrinsic value, trust the 10-K data over the analyst's narrative framing. The analyst's Watchlist verdict versus the primary-source Pass is also driven by the analyst treating the 20-25x multiple re-rating as a reasonable base case, while the primary-source framework does not credit unanchored multiple expansion as a reason to buy — particularly when the underlying capital allocation discipline that would justify a premium multiple is not present.

## Things to verify before acting

- Pull the fiscal 2025 10-K (or most recent 10-Q) and isolate Firefly usage-based revenue as a separate line item if disclosed, or find it in management commentary. The entire bull thesis depends on Firefly credits growing fast enough to offset seat-count pressure — if this figure is not being disclosed separately yet, that itself is a risk signal.
- Reconstruct the actual per-share FCF buyback math: take the $11.28B in fiscal 2025 repurchases and compare to the average repurchase price disclosed in the 10-K vs. the share price at the time. The analyst claims buybacks were at a 'depressed' price; the primary-source check disputes this. The 10-K will show weighted-average repurchase prices by quarter.
- Check the long-term debt schedule in the 10-K. If total senior notes outstanding have grown over the past 2-3 fiscal years while FCF has not proportionally expanded, the buyback program is partially debt-funded — a material capital allocation red flag that the analyst's card does not address.
- Listen to the most recent earnings call (Q4 FY2025 or Q1 FY2026) specifically for management's quantitative guidance on Digital Media ARR growth. The capital allocation concern is only redeemable if organic growth re-accelerates; management's own forward ARR guidance is the leading indicator.
- Run a simple downside scenario: if FCF compresses 15-20% from the fiscal 2025 $7.9B figure (reflecting seat-count pressure before Firefly offsets), what does the stock yield at current prices? At an 11.8% FCF yield today, a 15% FCF compression implies roughly a 10% yield — still attractive, but check whether that scenario changes your conviction level before sizing a position.
- Review the proxy statement to assess executive compensation structure. With insider ownership below 0.2% and no founder-operator dynamic, confirm whether the compensation plan is tied to per-share FCF growth or total FCF/revenue — if it is the latter, management has incentive to grow the denominator rather than optimize for per-share outcomes.
