## Summary

Adobe earns a Watchlist rating at a 7.40 weighted score, driven by genuinely exceptional owner earnings quality and a clean balance sheet, but held back by uncertainty around capital allocation discipline and AI-era moat durability. The business converts cash at a rate that exceeds net income — $10B operating cash flow against $7.1B net income — and carries only ~0.7x net debt/EBITDA, leaving it on structurally sound footing. The real tension is whether AI disrupts the creative-suite moat faster than Adobe can monetize Firefly and generative credits: the $9.5B+ in annual buybacks look aggressive if seat shrinkage accelerates and per-seat revenue doesn't offset. Valuation is a rare bright spot — a 9.1% FCF yield and a P/E near 14.7x against a 10-year median above 160x suggests the market has already priced in considerable disruption risk, making the entry price far more forgiving than Adobe's historical norm.

### What the disagreements mean

The sharpest disagreement between the analyst and primary sources is on buyback discipline: the analyst frames the $9.5B repurchase as a conviction signal, but the 10-K data shows repurchases materially exceeded operating cash flow, leaving net cash near zero — when sources disagree on a financial fact, the 10-K wins. The second disagreement is on revenue growth; the analyst asserts continued high-single to low-double-digit growth without providing current segment rates, while primary sources flag visible deceleration in public disclosures. Both gaps matter because the bull case depends on a multiple re-rating that requires accelerating, not slowing, growth.

## Things to verify before acting

- Check the capital allocation math directly: in Adobe's FY2025 10-K, reconcile total share repurchases ($11.28B) against operating cash flow ($10.0B) and net borrowings. Identify whether the excess was funded by new debt issuance or cash drawdown, and whether net cash has dropped below zero on a net-debt basis — this determines whether buybacks are sustainable at current pace.
- Pull the most recent earnings call transcript (FY2025 Q4 or Q1 FY2026) and find management's disclosed revenue growth rate by segment (Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, Digital Experience). Verify whether growth has already decelerated to mid-single digits; if so, the analyst's re-rating thesis requires scrutiny.
- On the AI moat question — the highest-uncertainty dimension — search for Canva's enterprise pricing page and any case studies of Canva displacing Adobe in mid-market creative teams. Also review Adobe's Firefly generative credit attach rate in any disclosed metric to assess whether consumption pricing is actually offsetting seat pressure.
- Reconcile the FCF multiple: the analyst cites 14x ex-SBC FCF; compute GAAP FCF by subtracting SBC (~$1.5–2B as disclosed in the 10-K cash flow statement) from operating cash flow to get the true unlevered FCF multiple you'd be paying. If it's closer to 17–18x, reassess the valuation case.
- Review the reverse-DCF gap manually: with a 9.1% FCF yield implying roughly $10B in FCF on a ~$110B market cap, back into what FCF CAGR is needed to justify the current price at a 10x terminal multiple and 10% hurdle rate. Compare that implied rate to Adobe's actual 5-year FCF CAGR from the 10-K to judge whether current pricing is generous or demanding.
- Monitor the Figma/design-tool competitive landscape: review any analyst notes from the past two quarters specifically on whether Figma's 2024 standalone momentum (post-merger termination) has accelerated into Adobe's UI/UX customer base, as this would be the first measurable sign of moat erosion in a segment Adobe disclosed it wanted to acquire.
