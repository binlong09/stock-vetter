## Summary

Intuit is a Watchlist candidate with a genuine moat in tax and SMB accounting software — switching costs are deep, the platform is structurally sticky, and revenue is largely recession-resistant given that tax filing is legally mandated. The score is held back by owner earnings quality and capital allocation: stock-based compensation consumes roughly $2B annually, share repurchases are effectively fully offset by SBC dilution delivering no per-share compounding, and the $12B Mailchimp acquisition is now shrinking. The GAAP EV/EBIT of 235x is a stark contrast to the more flattering 24-25x non-GAAP forward earnings multiple the market conversation centers on, and without a functioning reverse DCF the implied growth expectations embedded in today's price cannot be cleanly benchmarked. The business is high quality; the price requires the AI tailwinds, Mailchimp recovery, and Credit Karma cycle to all work out simultaneously.

### What the disagreements mean

The analyst video rates INTU a Pass at 5.0/10 while the primary-source checklist lands at 6.38/10 (Watchlist). The gap is mostly explained by how each source treats non-GAAP versus GAAP earnings: the analyst uses a 24x non-GAAP forward multiple as evidence of value, while the primary-source analysis weights the GAAP EV/EBIT of 235x and the economic cost of $2B in annual SBC far more heavily. On capital allocation and Mailchimp specifically, the analyst accepts management's forward narrative; the primary-source check is grounded in what the 10-K currently shows — flat share count despite $2.8B in buybacks and a shrinking $12B acquisition. Where sources disagree on a measurable fact (share count, Mailchimp revenue growth rate), trust the disclosed financials over the pitch framing.

## Things to verify before acting

- Pull Intuit's most recent 10-K and compute net share count change over three years: divide ending shares by beginning shares and compare to the capital returned in buybacks. If the share count is flat or rising despite $2-3B annual repurchases, SBC dilution is consuming all capital returned — this directly affects the owner earnings quality score.
- Check the Mailchimp goodwill carrying value on the balance sheet against the segment's current revenue trajectory. If the segment continues declining into FY2026 and management does not announce a recovery by Q2 FY2026 earnings, model a partial impairment scenario (e.g., 20-30% write-down) and assess the P&L impact, since this would hit GAAP earnings hard.
- Listen to the most recent earnings call (FY2025 Q4 or FY2026 Q1) specifically for management commentary on IRS Direct File expansion and competitive response. Direct File is the single most important exogenous risk to TurboTax revenue that is not fully priced into current models.
- Because the reverse DCF could not be computed for INTU, manually estimate it: take the current FCF yield of 4.81% (implying ~$3.1B owner FCF at current market cap), then back-solve what annual FCF growth rate over 10 years at a 10% discount rate is required to justify today's price. Compare that to Intuit's actual 5-year FCF CAGR from the 10-K to assess whether the market's implied expectations are realistic.
- Verify that the debt maturity schedule is not front-loaded: Intuit carries ~$6.0B in long-term notes and the debt sustainability score carried moderate uncertainty (range 1.5). Pull the maturity schedule from the 10-K notes to confirm no large refinancing cliff in the next 2-3 years, particularly given current interest rates versus when the debt was issued.
- Track the TurboTax Live 600-store buildout cadence and associated operating cost ramp in upcoming 10-Qs. The AI margin tailwind narrative only holds if the physical service layer costs scale sub-linearly with revenue — if operating costs in that segment grow faster than revenue, the margin expansion thesis breaks.
