## Moat durability

**Score:** 7.0 / 10   _samples: [7.0, 7.0, 7.0], range 0.0_ *(tight: low uncertainty)*

Intuit possesses a multi-dimensional moat built on data network effects (proprietary financial data across ~100 million customers powering AI), high switching costs embedded in deep workflow integration (QuickBooks is the accounting system of record for millions of SMBs, with payroll, payments, and tax all connected), and strong brand recognition across TurboTax and QuickBooks that has compounded for 40+ years. The platform flywheel — where each product feeds data into others and locks customers into the ecosystem — is a genuine structural advantage. However, Risk Factors explicitly acknowledge intensifying competitive threats from AI-native challengers, free government tax offerings (IRS Direct File), and large platform companies expanding into Intuit's markets, which prevent a higher score.

## Cyclicality awareness

However, the Credit Karma segment introduces meaningful cyclicality tied to consumer credit markets and financial institution partner activity, and macro conditions can affect small business formation and payment volumes. The risk factors explicitly acknowledge macro sensitivity but frame it as manageable rather than existential, and the tax business's mandatory-compliance nature provides a durable revenue floor.

### Citations
