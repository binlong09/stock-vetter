## Summary

META earns a Watchlist verdict, pulled in opposite directions by two dominant forces. On the positive side, the business has a genuine multi-layered moat — billions of users, an unmatched social graph, and advertiser network effects that compound over time — paired with founder-level alignment from Zuckerberg that is difficult to overstate. What keeps this off the Strong Candidate list is capital allocation uncertainty: $115–135B in annual capex guidance is compressing near-term FCF (currently yielding just 1.65%), and the returns on that AI infrastructure spend remain unproven. The reverse DCF is more constructive than it first appears — the market is pricing in only 11.6% annual FCF growth against a 5-year actual of 14.3% and a 3-year actual of 34.3%, so the stock isn't egregiously expensive on a normalized basis — but the path from here requires the capex cycle to deliver, and the FTC antitrust suit seeking forced divestiture of Instagram and WhatsApp represents a tail risk that is largely absent from consensus analysis.

## Things to verify before acting

- Track the FTC v. Meta antitrust case: check the current procedural posture (trial date, judge rulings) using federal court PACER or major legal news sources. If the case proceeds toward trial with a divestiture remedy still on the table, the moat thesis requires a serious probability-weighted haircut — this is the single risk most absent from bullish analyst coverage.
- When the 2025 10-K or 2026 Q1 10-Q is available, compare actual capex spending to the $115–135B annual guidance range. If actuals are tracking at or above the top of the range while revenue growth decelerates below 15%, the double-compression risk (FCF down, multiple down) flagged in the cyclicality dimension becomes live.
- Cyclicality scored 4.0 with moderate uncertainty (range of 1.0) — this is the highest-uncertainty dimension. Pull the most recent earnings call transcript (Q1 2026 when available) and listen specifically for management commentary on advertiser spending trends from SMBs, which are more macro-sensitive than large brand advertisers. A softening there is an early warning signal.
- Run the reverse DCF sensitivity yourself: at 10% discount and 20× terminal P/FCF, the market prices in 11.6% FCF CAGR. Ask what happens to intrinsic value if FCF goes flat for 2 years (capex peak) then resumes at 12% — model the 'valley' scenario, not just the normalized trend. This will tell you your actual margin of safety.
- Verify the $103.77B in uncommenced operating lease obligations cited in the capital allocation rationale against the 2024 10-K footnotes (look for the lease commitment table in the financial statements). This is a real off-balance-sheet liability that affects true capital deployment and is often excluded from adjusted metrics the analyst video relies on.
- Check SBC as a percentage of revenue for the last four quarters from the most recent 10-Q. SBC was flagged as a meaningful dilution risk in the owner earnings analysis — if it has risen above 8–9% of revenue, the gap between GAAP net income and true owner earnings widens, and any buyback math needs to be adjusted accordingly.
- Look up Reality Labs cumulative losses since 2019 in the segment footnotes and calculate total capital destroyed to date versus any disclosed monetization milestones. If losses are accelerating without a clear product milestone (Quest unit volumes, spatial computing revenue), use this as a contra-indicator on overall capital allocation quality.
