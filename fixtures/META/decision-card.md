## Things to verify before acting

- Track the FTC v. Meta antitrust trial closely — the case seeking forced divestiture of Instagram and WhatsApp is the single highest-consequence tail risk not reflected in most valuation models. Read the current trial docket and any summary judgment rulings before sizing a position.
- When META's next 10-Q lands, compare actual capex spend against the $115–135B annual guidance range. If actual capex is tracking above the high end of guidance, the FCF compression will be worse than the 11.6% implied CAGR in the reverse DCF assumes, and the 1.65% FCF yield will deteriorate further.
- Demand evidence for the 16% capex ROIC claim before accepting it. Look in earnings call transcripts and investor day materials for any disclosed framework: incremental revenue attributable to AI infrastructure, payback periods, or segment-level return disclosures. If no derivation exists, treat the 16% as an unsubstantiated assertion.
- Check Reality Labs cumulative losses and 2025 operating loss trajectory in the 10-K. The segment has consumed over $100B in capital with negligible commercial return. Assess whether management's disclosed milestones for Orion and the metaverse represent credible near-term monetization or continued speculative burn.
- Run the price target math yourself: $35 adjusted EPS times 18x equals roughly $630, near the current price. Reaching $875 requires either higher EPS or a multiple closer to 25x. Decide independently whether multiple expansion is plausible given the capex overhang and the historical 10-year median P/E of 25.8.
- Review the $20.4B in annual stock-based compensation relative to the buyback program. META repurchased $26.26B in shares in 2025, but SBC dilution offsets a meaningful portion of that return. Calculate the net share count reduction over the past three years to assess true capital return to owners.
- Listen to the most recent earnings call and any CFO commentary on advertising revenue sensitivity to macro conditions. Given that nearly 100% of revenue is advertising-dependent, assess whether forward guidance has been stress-tested against a mild recession scenario, and compare that to the cyclicality dimension score of 5.0.
